Cognify — Speaker Notes

Print this. Keep it on the table. Do not read verbatim — use it as a prompt.

Total runtime: ~2 minutes · 7 slides · ~17 seconds each
Slide 01 — Title ~15 sec

"We built tools to track our physical fitness. We built tools to track our sleep. But nobody has built a tool to measure cognitive fitness — the combination of attention quality and intellectual independence — in the age of AI. That's Cognify."

Pause after "That's Cognify." Let it land. Don't rush into the next slide.
Slide 02 — The Problem ~20 sec

"Here's the problem. We consume more information than ever — mostly through AI — and we get zero feedback on how well we're processing it. 65% of health misinformation is accepted in conversational form. AI makes it worse because it's authoritative in tone. People trust it, and they have no idea where their own thinking breaks down."

Point to the "65%" stat on screen. Business jury: frame it as a market pain, not just a social problem.
Slide 03 — The World is Shifting ~20 sec

"Look at the wellness tech trajectory. People pay $30 a month for a WHOOP to know their physical recovery score. They pay $16 for an Oura Ring to know their sleep stages. The quantified self market is $70 billion — and cognitive performance is the one frontier nobody has touched yet. The category is ours to define."

Emphasise "nobody has touched yet" — this is the white space argument the business jury will respond to.
Slide 04 — The Product ~20 sec

"Cognify is simple. You paste any conversation with an AI assistant. It scores your critical thinking, flags claims you accepted without questioning — like this health claim flagged as high risk — and over time it builds a cognitive fingerprint: a plain-English narrative of your epistemic habits and blind spots."

If demoing live: switch to the app tab now and click Demo. Let the scores animate. Then switch back. If not demoing: point to the score numbers and the HIGH RISK badge on this slide.
Slide 05 — Business Model ~20 sec

"The model is subscription — exactly like WHOOP. WHOOP charges $30 a month to tell you your recovery score. We charge $15 a month to tell you your cognitive fitness score. Free tier keeps users in the funnel. The real money is enterprise and education — one university licensing deal is worth thousands of individual subscribers."

Business jury focus: stress the enterprise tier. Recurring B2B revenue with per-seat pricing is the high-margin story. Freemium is acquisition, not revenue.
Slide 06 — Market ~15 sec

"180 million weekly AI assistant users. $70 billion wellness market. One in three employers now requires AI literacy as a hiring factor. The consumer habit of paying for self-tracking already exists. We're the first product at the intersection of all three."

Keep this fast — the numbers speak for themselves. Don't elaborate. Move to the close.
Slide 07 — Close ~10 sec

"We tracked our steps. We tracked our sleep. Nobody tracked how well we thought — until now. The market is there. The timing is right. The product is live. That's Cognify."

Say "The product is live" with confidence — you have a working app. End on "That's Cognify." Smile. Stop talking.

General reminders

For the business/marketing judges specifically: stress the WHOOP comparison on slide 5, the white space framing on slide 3, and the enterprise tier revenue model. They want to see a clear monetisation path — you have one.

If asked about competition: "No one is doing this. Fact-checkers check content. We check the thinker. Different category."

If asked about data/AI accuracy: "The extraction engine runs on Claude. The schema is fixed — structured output, not freeform. Accuracy improves with more sessions."

If asked about privacy: "Everything is stored locally in the browser. No server, no database, no data leaving the device in the current build."
