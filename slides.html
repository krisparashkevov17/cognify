How It Works

Three layers of analysis.


1

Cognitive Scoring

Every conversation is scored across three dimensions: Critical Thinking (do you challenge claims?), Depth (how deeply do you explore?), and Engagement (how actively do you participate?). Scores are 0–100 and visualised as circular indicators.

2

Claim Risk Mapping

Each significant claim in the conversation is extracted, categorised by topic, and assigned a risk level. High Risk marks unverified or dangerous claims. Medium flags contested or oversimplified ones. Verified confirms well-supported positions.

3

Longitudinal Profile + Fingerprint

Across sessions, a cumulative profile emerges: topic frequency map, trend line, blind spots (where you consistently accept high-risk claims), strengths, and a second-person cognitive fingerprint — a narrative paragraph describing your epistemic habits.

Societal Relevance

Why this matters beyond the individual.


Public Health

Health misinformation is the WHO's leading "infodemic" risk. Cognify is the first tool that can show individuals exactly where their skepticism is underactive — before a bad decision is made.

Education

Schools teach content. Cognify teaches process. Imagine a student seeing their critical thinking score improve over a semester — concrete, measurable epistemic growth.

Democracy & Media

Misinformation thrives on uncritical acceptance. Giving people a personal accountability metric for how they engage with claims creates a structural incentive for better reasoning.

The AI Risk Angle

As AI assistants become primary knowledge interfaces, human over-reliance is a documented risk. Cognify makes that dependency visible and actionable — not by limiting AI, but by strengthening the human half of the loop.
